# Professional Self-Assessment

Throughout my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science program, I have developed technical skills and professional abilities that have prepared me to enter the field as a well-rounded computing professional. Completing coursework and creating this ePortfolio allowed me to reflect on the growth I have experienced and demonstrate the knowledge and skills I have gained. The process of enhancing previous projects for my capstone strengthened my understanding of software development practices and reinforced the importance of writing maintainable, secure, and efficient code. In addition to the artifacts included in this portfolio, coursework in web development, secure coding, embedded systems, and software engineering expanded my experience with a variety of programming languages, tools, and technologies.

Throughout the program, I developed skills in collaboration and communication that are essential in professional environments. Many assignments required considering the needs of different audiences and documenting technical solutions clearly. Through project reports, presentations, and software documentation, I learned how to communicate technical information to stakeholders in ways that support decision making. My experience as a manager and team leader outside of computer science also strengthened my ability to work effectively with diverse groups, resolve problems, and contribute to team success.

The program provided extensive experience with algorithms, data structures, software engineering, databases, and security. I learned how to design efficient solutions while evaluating trade-offs between different approaches. Coursework involving object-oriented programming, web applications, and embedded systems strengthened my understanding of software design principles and development practices. Database projects introduced concepts such as data modeling, storage, and retrieval, while secure coding assignments emphasized input validation, error handling, encryption, and protecting systems against common vulnerabilities. These experiences helped me develop a security mindset and recognize the importance of designing software that protects both data and users.

The artifacts included in this ePortfolio demonstrate the range of knowledge and skills I developed throughout the program. The text-based adventure game highlights improvements in algorithms, modular design, and code maintainability. The Raspberry Pi thermostat project demonstrates software engineering principles, state-machine design, and embedded programming concepts. The weight tracking application showcases database integration and application development through the use of Java and Firebase. Together, these artifacts illustrate how different areas of computer science complement one another and represent my growth as a developer. This portfolio reflects not only the technical knowledge I have gained, but also my commitment to continuous learning, problem solving, and producing secure, reliable, and professional software solutions.
